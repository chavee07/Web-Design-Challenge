Cloud Cover versus Latitude





Analysis & Observations: Another weather indicator is the relative cloud cover, as measured by the percent coverage by clouds at a geographical location. It is well documented that tropical regions have higher annual rainfall, and that the majority of tropical localities are closer to the Equator. Knowing this, the Cloudiness plot does not seem to have any correlation to latitude. One can say however, that there seems to be a collection of points across all latitudes at either 0%, 75% or 100% Cloudy.However, the assumption that this would correlate with cloud coverage is not supported by the weather data. For further analysis we can look into whether or not the measure is subjective or objective.
