Temperature versus Latitude





Analysis & Observations: We can assume that weather in latitudes closer to the Equator(lower latitudes) have higher maximum temperatures when compared to weather in higher latitudes. Here we can address this assuption by correlating the latitudes for over 500 random cities with maximum temperatures for a specific date. The use of 01/05/2017 is still winter in the Northern Hemisphere and Summer in the Southern Hemisphere. We would expect the Southern Latitudes to have higher temperatures than the Northern Latitudes and the latitudes nearer the Equator to have the highest maximum temperatures. These predictions were confirmed by the weather data, showing higher maximum temperatures as latitude approaches the Equator (Latitude=0). Maximum temperatures in the Southern Hemisphere (Summer) were also observed to be elevated compared to maximum temperatures in the Northern Hemisphere (Winter). We see there was a decline in temperatures to around -50 degress(F) in the Northern Hemi. as we get further away from 0 latitude. As for the Southern Hemi. we see a drop in temperatures the further we go south from the equator, but the change of temperatures is smaller compared to the Northern Hemi. and its lowest is around 30 degrees (F). For future correlative experiments we can consider using dates where Summer and Winter are inverted compared to these (Summer in the Northern Hemisphere and Winter in the Southern Hemisphere).
